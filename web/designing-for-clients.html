Designing for Clients

Fri, 26 Oct 2012

Posted In: web

Tagged: clientsstrategypersonal

I recently submitted an initial website design for one of our Opt(e)Web clients to review. The design uses a somewhat unconventional layout, but it has some interesting possibilities for this client, and I think it has the potential to be visually engaging.

But I'm not sure the client likes it that well:

  • The layout, as I said, is a little different.
  • I think the client is having difficulty imagining their information in the mockup I gave them; this may partly be my fault
  • There may be a few "little things" that are getting in the way: the client may not like a particular placeholder image I chose, or they may want some different colors here and there. This ALWAYS happens with every project; I don't think any good designer can deliver the perfect product on the first round.

The web design process has a lot of challenges, and designing for clients is difficult for many reasons. I usually have an incomplete understanding of my clients' businesses and their personal aesthetic tastes don't always match mine. A big part of the job is getting inside my clients' heads. It's not enough to build a site that looks like their business card - instead, I have to craft a design that will meet their business needs, represent who they are, and present (usually) a lot of information in a way that is informative and useful to website users.

So when I try something new - such as this website layout - I have to make sure it will work for the client. Sometimes I guess wrong. I'm still holding out hope for the design in this situation, albeit with some modifications. And, as usual, there's something to learn from this project.

What can I do differently?

I should have filled the mockup (it's a coded site) with information and pictures that relate to the client's site. Even though I don't have any of their actual content yet, I should have take more time to find some related text and photos.

Maybe in this case, I ought to have called the client instead of sending an email with the mockup link. That's a really tough call, since you have to know how best to communicate with specific clients.

When I talk to the client, I'll try to talk up the possibilities and potential I see in the design.

But it's important to remember not to get too personally invested in a design concept. It's not my design; it's my client's design.
