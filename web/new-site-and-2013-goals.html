New site and 2013 Goals

Sat, 05 Jan 2013

Posted In: web

Tagged: newspersonal

Happy New Year!

Welcome to the new and improved AJY.co! This is the time of year that one redesigns one's website and ponders the year ahead.

What's coming up in 2013?

2012 was a very busy year for me. You may know that I'm the Design Director for Opt(e)Web, a small tech company here in Salem, Oregon. Business ramped up steadily throughout the year and we have been challenged to improve our approach to project management and client communication. We've made huge strides and I'm working more efficiently than I ever have. But there's a lot going on, and it doesn't leave me much time for self-improvement, networking, and sometimes a personal life.

From a professional perspective, 2013 is a year of focusing on the improvements I need to make. I'm hoping that my blogging here and over at the Opt(e)Web site can spur me to improve my networking with other web professionals in our area and beyond. So you should expect to see frequent, if not regular, updates on both websites.

At http://opt-e-web.com I will be posting web-related subjects and tips that I encounter in my day-to-day work life. These tips will be focused on one of two audiences: either our customers who, in some cases, may not be particularly web-savvy; or our colleagues in this industry.

Here on this site I will be posting about a wider range of subjects that might interest me. I hope to write about web-related topics as well as Linux and software topics (I'm kind of a Linux hobbyist). Finally, I'll post personal-related blog posts from time to time. FYI, I do have category-specific RSS/Atom feeds.

Also coming up in 2013

  • I am hoping to get back on the bike and in great shape. Time will tell if I'm able to work it into my regular schedule.
  • I want to cook more
  • I want to continue getting better at project management and working efficiently
  • I want to network better in the web industry
  • I want to help/mentor an up-and-coming web designer/developer in the area. At this point I don't have time for a full-fledged mentoring program, but occasional coffee meetings would be great.

About my new website design

I'm going to go bulleted-list style on this section as well. I plan on writing about many of these features in future blog posts, so I'll just mention them here:

  • The most significant change to my website is actually under the hood. I have switched from Wordpress to Pelican, a static blogging engine. Basically, I have a folder full of Markdown files on my computer. Each text file is a post or page of my website; when I write a new post, I run Pelican and it regenerates my site. It creates static HTML files, so my website should be pretty fast since there's no database involved.
  • New design, of course. Make my logo and name smaller, people aren't here to stare at them. Use more cutting-edge technologies - the logo is SVG, for example.
  • This is kind of fun. Notice that if you scroll down, the header stays fixed at the top of the page. The content section of the website fades to the background photo just under the header. This is a trick I'll definitely write about soon, because I haven't seen anything quite like this elsewhere.
  • Also, if you scroll down, try clicking on a blank spot in the dark gray header. It'll scoot you right back up to the top of the page. This was just a little tricky and I'll write about it later as well.
  • I've dropped about 3/4 of the posts from my old website. I've kept posts that I think may be relevant or posts that have been attracting a decent amount of traffic.
  • Still to do: I'm getting a website search function going pretty soon. You'll see it at the top of the right sidebar.

Come along for 2013!

  • Twitter - I'm fairly active on Twitter too.
  • RSS / Atom - Subscribe to my feed to get updates in your feed reader.
  • Contact Me directly or comment below with your questions, comments, and suggestions.
