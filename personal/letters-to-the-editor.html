Letters to the Editor

Mon, 10 Jan 2011

Posted In: personal

Tagged: photodelicious

OK, time for a quick fun post that has nothing to do with anything.

Over time, I've collected a few funny letters to the editor. All the ones shown here are from the World in Coos Bay, Oregon. I decided it was time to share some. Enjoy!

Letter 1: Editor is in the Jaws of Stupidity

"Jaws of Stupidity" - now there's a good metaphor. I've got to remember to use that one sometime. This letter, which appears to be in response to an editorial by Kathy Erickson (the then-editor of the newspaper), is very strongly written but doesn't really appear to say anything. Not anything comprehensible, at least.

Also worth noting: That wasn't a great day for letters all around. So click the image below for BIGGER! I didn't want to crop out the other letters.

Letter 2: What really caused the Feb. 7th storm?

I'm not sure what kind of world this guy thinks he lives in. I wonder what Peter DeFazio thought.

Letter 3: Enjoying a sunny, soothing interlude

Ah, a breath of fresh air and positivity in the midst of so much turmoil. I'm glad this person is happy and carefree. I was just thinking, when I wake up in the morning and I'm in a good mood, the first thing I want to do is write a letter to the editor to tell 60,000 people that I'm happy.

Letter 4: A Bavarian eatery would be nice in North Bend

It seems there's a fairly sizeable group of people in North Bend (probably mostly in the 85+ age group) that feel that their city is perfect and Coos Bay is constantly plotting to take over (consolidating the two cities has appeared on several ballots and would probably make economic sense for both cities and the larger region).

This person, though, has found something that North Bend needs: a Bavarian restaurant. Apparently, they can prove that there is no Bavarian restaurant by listing EVERY OTHER kind of restaurant that IS in North Bend. The overall effect is that this person had a little trouble staying on topic.
