New Salem bridge!

Thu, 09 Apr 2009

Posted In: personal

Tagged: salem

The old railroad bridge over the Willamette River has been converted to a pedestrian/bike bridge and is open now! It connects Riverfront Park and Wallace Marine Park. Riverfront Park is a grassy park with a carousel and wide concrete walking paths, and lots of community festivals and things are held there; Wallace Marine is a little more wild and has a reputation as the location of several homeless camps. Hopefully the increased foot and bike traffic in the area - along with city and volunteer efforts to clean out some undergrowth and maintain running trails in the park - will make the park a more pleasant place to be.

As you can see, the bridge isn't much of a looker, but it's nice to have.

Even though it doesn't look beautiful from a distance, it's not unpleasant when you're there.

There's a lead paint issue on the bridge, it's flaking off on places like this. It's been sealed 6-8 feet above the walkway, the picture here shows an area above that. As far as I know, the bridge will be closed over next winter so the issue can be taken care of.

Hey, Salem's got something cool now! In your FACE, Bend!
