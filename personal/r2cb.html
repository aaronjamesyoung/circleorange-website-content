R2CB!

Wed, 22 Apr 2009

Posted In: personal

Roseburg to Coos Bay Relay was last weekend. I ran on a team with my family (all except David). Here's how things went:

  1. Drive to Coos Bay on Friday afternoon and watch the Coos County track meet. Eat some dinner.
  2. Wake up at 4:00 a.m. This run had better be a LOT of fun.
  3. Leave at 4:30 a.m. to drive to Roseburg in time for our 7:00 start.
  4. Start! My mom takes leg 1 out toward Lookingglass in the cool overcast weather. At least it's dry. Leg 1 is mostly uphill.
  5. We see a jeep drive by like three times during leg 2 (Dad) and leg 3 (Steph). On my leg (4), I see it being towed.
  6. I run leg 4. Still cloudy and cold. Leg 4 is flat at first, then starts going up... it goes up for a while, then downhill, then up again on a steeper hill. Overall not an easy leg, but I felt pretty good and did fine powering up the hill.
  7. Corey has leg 5. Ha ha ha ha ha! This is a nasty hill.
  8. Mom takes over on leg 6. This finishes off the hill from leg 5 and then goes downhill on the other side. The Coquille River starts up here, as a little trickle becoming a stream. The road changes to gravel and the clouds cleared off on this leg. For the rest of the run, it was sunny and really warm, although it cooled off a bit as we approached the coast of course.
  9. As Dad is running leg 7, we're driving down the gravel road in the middle of the forest, with waterfalls along the ever-growing Coquille River.
  10. We pick up some pavement on Steph's downhill leg 8.
  11. My leg 9 is flat, short, and hot. I didn't feel very good on this one. Not looking forward to the next one.
  12. After Corey's leg, Mom has a fun leg: 2 miles up, 2 miles down. Another runner was looking at the gentle slope rising from the exchange point, she said, "is it like this the whole way up?" Not really... she reaches the top of the hill spewing expletives... Mom says, "well, the last half mile was pretty steep, I guess".
  13. Fairview! Everyone's stopping at the store since it's the first one we've seen for hours. But Coos Bay is just over the hill..
  14. Up Blue Ridge, and down the other side on the gravel road to Sumner. Almost time for me to run my third leg...
  15. I get to run my last leg on the rolling hills from Sumner to the big hill I used to run several times a week to get to my house... I felt great, charged over the top of the big hill and down Ross Inlet Road.
  16. Corey finishes off by running into Eastside then Coos Bay and finishing at Freddy's.
  17. Time to get some 'za at Abby's. We were third in our division in 9:27:26.
